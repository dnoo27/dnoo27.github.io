Who do credit unions belong to?

The disengaged disproportionately dislike credit unions. While political disengagement is a societal problem, it seems to have little direct relevance for credit unions. After all, credit unions are nonpartisan organizations that, for the most part, stay out of the political fray to the extent they can. Credit unions pride themselves on being broad-based, mission-driven organizations that meet the needs of the entire community regardless of any individual’s political affiliation. And in fact, past research has shown that credit unions are not partisan institutions, and citizens do not view them as such. They are widely appreciated and supported by Democrats and Republicans alike.[1]
